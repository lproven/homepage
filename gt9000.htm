Epson GT-9000

Price: £759

Supplier: Epson 0800-220546

Verdict: Higher resolution and faster than the 8500, but the end result isn't greatly better quality.

The final Epson this month is the high-end GT-9000. Slightly larger than the 8500, this is an imposing creature, with a striking blue hinge mechanism to its lid, which even as a document pocket on top, a neat and friendly touch. Another neat detail unique to this model is a fold-out platform on the right hand side, for supporting wide originals. Around the back are a similar set of ports to the 8500; the 9000 can use the same sheetfeeder and transparency adaptor as the smaller model. With a true 600x600ppi optical resolution, this unit is moderately well suited to scanning transparencies - 300ppi scanners will only make a small, grainy image from a 35mm slide, whilst dedicated slide scanners run at thousands of ppi. The colour resolution is the same 30 bits as the 8500, however, and whilst it did slightly better in our colour accuracy test, results in this and the photo tests were disappointing. Epson seems to have optimised the scanner's performance, though, and it was measurably faster than the 8500.

Epson's high level of attention to details shows in all three machines. As well as power switches located conveniently at the front of the case, they all have reset buttons as well. These are seldom needed, but scanning large, high-resolution photos can generate huge image files which strain PCs to the limit. If your program crashes, it may be necessary to reset the scanner before trying again, and the Epsons make this easy. The same drivers are supplied as with the other two, and the same Adaptec card as with the 8500, so the same comments apply.

The software bundle with all three scanners is impressive: image editing is provided by a full copy of Corel PhotoPaint 5. This is the latest 16-bit version of Corel's retouching package, and has an impressive range of features; it comes complete with the Mosaic add-in for image file management. An incongruous but pleasant extra is a CD containing the older CorelDRAW! 4 suite. Document image processing and OCR is provided by the Xerox Color [sic] Document Management package, including TextBridge OCR. This supplies tools for scanning, OCRing, filing, searching and sorting documents, but unfortunately, in our tests, the 16-bit version didn't function correctly under Windows 95, with TextBridge reporting that no scanner was installed; Windows 95 users might be better advised to upgrade to TextBridge Pro 96 (reviewed in PC Pro issue 23) or OmniPage Pro.

Unfortunately, in our tests, the Epson didn't do as well as we expected. Although it's a reasonable and flexible machine, it doesn't offer enough benefits over the 8500 unless you really need the higher resolution.
