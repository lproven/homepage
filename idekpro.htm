Iiyama MT-9017E VisionMaster Pro 17

Verdict: Not the finest example of this model PC Pro has seen, but still one of the best on test.

Price: £649

Supplier: Iiyama 01483-745482

This, the second Iiyama this month, is the more sophisticated Pro version. It comes equipped with a Mitsubishi Diamondtron aperture-grill screen, as opposed to the more conventional shadow-mask of the standard model.

Aside from the Diamondtron screen, there's little practical difference from the more basic model; the casing and controls are unchanged. Aperture grill screens are only curved horizontally, so the screen is flatter and has 15mm more visible area, and the image is slightly sharper due to the finer 0.25mm grill pitch. The on-screen menus and adjustments are the same, which is a pity - the more expensive screen particularly would benefit from additional, finer controls, such as linearity and bow. The slim manual is shared by both models, with special entries for the areas where they differ. It's terse, but covers most of the important areas - although it omits the handy troubleshooting tip of checking the video card is correctly fitted.

Like the other Iiyama, when using the BNC connectors, Windows 95 can't detect the monitor type. However, even with a D-Sub cable, the Pro model isn't on Windows' list of monitor types; Iiyama have a "driver", a small MONITOR.INF file which tells Windows the resolutions it can display, but this wasn't supplied: we had to fetch it from Iiyama's web site. With this, you also gain the handy 1152x864 mode.

All monitors vary slightly, even individual ones from the same range, and this is one of the poorer VisionMaster Pros we've seen. It needed slight adjustments to its geometry and linearity, but it lacks the necessary controls, so it fell down slightly in testing; this is a pity, as many manufacturers supply Iiyamas and we've seen plenty of them. Nonetheless, it's a very good price for a Trinitron-type screen, and is worth buying.
