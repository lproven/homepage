NEC MultiSync XE17

Verdict: Large but attractive, with good controls, but disappointing performance and specification for the price.

Price: £999

The Nippon Electric Corporation invented the multisync monitor, and have registered the word itself as their trademark, so we expected something a little special, and we got it: the only 17in monitor in this Labs with a maximum rated resolution of 1024x768, the minimum most people would expect of a 17in screen. Windows 95 correctly detected and identified the monitor on startup, and the we couldn't select any higher resolution. Overriding the Windows monitor with Matrox's monitor selection utility allowed higher resolution modes of 1152x864 and 1280x1024, but both were at a rather flickery 60Hz. Within its rated range, it's a lot better, and at 1024x768 the image is clear and stable, but fine detail is poor, perhaps because of the comparitively coarse 0.28mm dot pitch.

The XE17 is an imposing beast, with a huge, flat frame around the CRT itself. The controls aren't recessed, but are presented as neat little buttons below the screen, with eight separate buttons to make controlling the excellent and helpful on-screen menus easy. There are also separate wheels for controlling brightness and contrast, though these don't have the usual "sticking point" to indicate the centre of the range, and the brightness control's range is small.

Swinging the unit around on its stiff stand, the back is unusually attractive: curved, and devoid of ventilation slots or large stickers, it looks as good from either side - ideal for a reception desk or anywhere where it's rear is on view. The cables aren't recessed very far, but the curvature means it will still fit up against a wall if necessary. Snags for some people will be the absence of BNC connectors, and a non-switching power supply. All things considered, this is a disappointing screen for such a big name and big price.
