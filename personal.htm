Although my BSc is in Biology, I found that people will pay me much more
to play with computers all day. Other interests include reading - mainly
science fiction, bikes (with and without engines), music (all sorts with
the exception of folk and country; I also make appalling noises with the
didgeridu and mouthorgan), languages and linguistics (dabble in French -
struggle in German and Spanish - can make polite noises in Norwegian and
Swedish), travel (mainly Europe and Africa so far).

I was born in Ormskirk, just outside Liverpool - born Lancastrian, but a
Liverpudlian by early upbringing. I left with all possible speed - first
to Nigeria for about 8 years, then a brief spell in Southport, thence to
the Isle of Man for ten years, three of these studying at Royal Holloway
and Bedford New College, University of London, in Egham, Surrey.

Professionally, I do consultancy and technical support on computers - by
which I mean that I recommend or specify, install, configure, customise,
train and support both standalone and network systems.

I am platform-agnostic. I mainly work with Windows (all versions) on PCs
but I also do Macintosh and Linux and a host of obsolete systems.

Alas, I'm a lousy programmer, but I fiddle fractals, artificial life and
suchlike. A long time ago I did fractal generators for various computers
under the name “Heathercliffe Software” but I gave up when FractInt came
out. A current low-priority background task is rewriting the HS suite of
programs as a Win32 screensaver in VB. If this interests you, please let
me know!

I also work as a freelance computer journalist. I've been published in a
variety of titles, including PC Pro, Network News, PC Magazine, MacUser,
PCW, PC Advisor, and Vector (the magazine of the British Science Fiction
Association - http://www.bsfa.co.uk). My specialities are networks (LANs
to the Internet) and operating systems, but I'll take any job with money
at the end. Reviews and feature work are always welcome - and I can work
to very short deadlines if necessary! :¬)

In connection with this, I also do talks, to live audiences and a little
radio work.

Personally, I'm a tall, dark figure given to lurking in the dark corners
of nightclubs, loud concerts by little-known bands, SF conventions, real
ale festivals, and custom bike shows. I seem to get regularly accused of
being a Goth - except by Goths.

I am also a biker. Until the 27th of May 1994, I rode a beautiful Kwack:
a de-restricted Kawasaki ZZR1100-C1 (with a custom paintjob in the style
of a GSX-R1100). On my way up to Norwich for the bank holiday weekend, I
was knocked off it by a coach while overtaking a traffic jam. The driver
(one Mr Thomas Rigg) was prosecuted for careless driving and fined £200,
which doesn't make up for the two months I spent in hospital with a left
leg broken at the ankle (3×), tibia (2×), fibula (2×), femur (multiple),
hip, right humerus (shoulder joint detached), left shoulder (dislocated)
and inner-ear damage from whiplash.

I am now held together by quite a lot of surgical steel rods and screws,
and glad (as well as EXTREMELY lucky) to be alive at all.

It was all going so well, too. Good job, loads of money, two girlfriends
- Danie in Norwich and Kim in London - bike of my dreams, a fun place to
live. 3 months later, no girlfriends, no bike, semi-crippled, and living
alone. C'est la vie. It's better now. Not quite that good, granted, but…

The ZZR still exists; it's being rebuilt as a three-wheeler by The Trike
Shop in Cardiff (it should resemble the big Hayabusa, but in purple.)

I also ride a Linear recumbent bicycle: a 2m beam with a little wheel on
the front and a big one at the back, with me lying on top. This has been
clocked at a smidge over 40mph on the flat and 70mpg downhill.
