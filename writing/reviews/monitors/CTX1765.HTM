CTX 1765D

Verdict: A modest but very reasonable screen for a bargain price

Price: £449

Supplier: CTX 01923-818461

CTX - sometimes also known as Chuntex - monitors are a familiar sight in PC Pro Labs; its 15in models are rebadged by many suppliers such as Amstrad and Dan. It submitted three screens this month: the 1765D, 1785S and 1785XA. The manuals refer to a 1785D and 1765S too, so they have quite a range. The 1765D is a basic model: it has a shadow-mask screen, and unusally this month, no on-screen menus, relying on four pairs of control buttons instead. These are simple and intuitive to use, although more specialised adjustments require you to press the mode button first, which isn't obvious. Omitting the extra circuitry for on-screen menus should reduce the cost somewhat.

The 65 or 85 on the end of the model numbers denote the dot pitch: the 65s are 0.28mm, whilst the 85s are 0.26mm. Although this model has the most basic CRT, image quality is good. Like most, it couldn't show 1-pixel vertical lines accurately at 1024x768, but focus and resolution were otherwise good. It had problems with moiré patterning on large dithered areas, and there's no adjustment available for this. Also, it exhibited streaking and ghosting on high-contrast screens, but this isn't uncommon and is seldom intrusive. There's little else to criticise, but the anti-reflective coating isn't much use: on a dark screen, the operator can clearly see their own reflection.

The casing is plain, angular and quite nondescript: the adjustment buttons are in a groove beneath the screen, and the back is a plain square with a label in the middle. The cables are neatly recessed, but the captive signal lead is a common drawback on budget models. The stand pivots a full 90° in either direction, which is good.

The sacrifice of on-screen menus is worth it - you get a lot of screen for your money, and its quality is enough for general use.
