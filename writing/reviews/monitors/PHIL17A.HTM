Phillips Brilliance 17A

Verdict: Good design, but average image quality and lousy sound for the price.

Price: £999

Supplier: 0181-689-4444

Philips' entrant this month is interesting: it is one of the few "multimedia monitors" on test, in that it includes a pair of stereo loudspeakers, though no microphone. The speakers are amplified, but the sound quality is very harsh and tinny, and they are no substitute for a seperate pair for any except the most basic usage.

The screen itself is a shadow-mask design, with a fine 0.26mm dot pitch, but its performance on fine detail is disappointing, especially in the corners, and it suffers badly from moiré effects. Impressively, it can display a steady 1280x1024 image at 75Hz, which is too high for this size of screen to accurately resolve individual pixels, but might be useful occasionally. It can also sustain 1600x1200, but this is very flickery and small details are very hard to see. At a more moderate 1024x768, the image is stable and sharp.

It is well-supplied with controls: wheels for contrast, brightness and volume. Although the brightness doesn't go as high as most monitors, it's enough, whilst the volume goes far higher than the speakers can manage. There's a headphone jack on the front panel, too, which is very useful in as busy office, and far preferable to fumbling for the sound card's. Other display adjustments are via on-screen menus, but they only cover the main options, and the menu graphics are very chunky - they badly need finer detail and clearer explanations.

All round, this is an attractive monitor with a decent display, but the speakers are awful, and the display isn't nearly good enough to justify the thousand-pound pricetag.
