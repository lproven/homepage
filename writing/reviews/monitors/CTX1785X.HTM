CTX 1785XA

Verdict: A keen price for a Diamondtron screen, but let down by poor quality control.

Price: £599

Supplier: CTX 01923-818461

This is the final member of the trio of CTXs, and the top of their 17in line. The specifications sound good: an aperture-grill CRT with a fine 0.26mm dot pitch, a high maximum resolution of 1600x1200 pixels, control via on-screen menus, BNC connectors... the works. It even looks more expensive than the D and S models, with a different, curvier case, and the touch-pad controls hidden on a drop-down panel. And then you turn it on. The 85XA is one of the few screens to fail our background interference test, with two large areas of discolouration at left and right, and smaller ones in two corners. The focus tests show that the discoloured areas are matched by larger areas of very bad focus, where the pixels are blurred. To be fair, where the picture is clear, it's very sharp and clear - but that's only half of the screen. The only thing that's clear all over the screen is the operator's reflection. Obviously, this screen's more than usually defective, or the range wouldn't be on sale. However, PC Pro's sister magazine MacUser was also conducting a monitor labs at the same time as us, and we examined their CTX 1785XA which exhibited similar problems, so it's not unique.

The poor quality of the display is a shame, because otherwise this is a pleasing monitor: the build quality is generally good, with helpful controls and a shapely

case with cables that, though not recessed by very much, shelter under an overhanging rear. The stand has an easy action and swivels just over 90° in each direction, which is exceptional. If you are able to check the quality of the picture on your individual unit, it should be a good buy, but if not, take care.
