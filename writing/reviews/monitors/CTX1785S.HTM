CTX 1785S

Verdict: £50 more gets you a higher-spec screen and menu controls, but not a significantly better image.

Price: £499

Supplier: CTX 01923-818461

This is the second of our trio of CTX screens, representing one of their mid-range units. It comes in the same case as the 1765D, complete with captive signal lead and no BNC connectors. The main differences are that the 85 models have a finer 0.26mm dot pitch screen which can run at up to 1600x1200, and the S which indicates on-screen menu controls. These are good: there are separate keys to enter and exit the menus, along with up/down and plus/minus adjusters. You don't have to enter submenus to adjust each parameter, which makes using CTX's menus faster than most others, such as Iiyama's fiddly equivalents.

The display itself is a bit of a disappointment; it suffers from linearity and moiré problems, for which there are no adjustments. Despite the fine dot pitch, the image lacks sharpness and clarity; although it's not bad enough to fail our tests, it doesn't look as crisp as some others. When the brightness was adjusted correctly according to our test software, it was right on its minimum, and the screen looked quite dim - signs that the sub-brightness scale needed adjustment, which also isn't on the menus.

On the positive side, the monitor was correctly recognised by Windows, and gave a stable, flicker-free image at all 1024x768, 1152x864, 1280x1024 and 1600x1200, which is impressive. The higher two modes only sustained 65Hz, but this is still good for a 17-incher. The slim manual is helpful and informative, although there are only minor differences between the booklets for the three monitors - CTX might be better advised to produce one general manual, like NEC.

The additional cost of this over the 65D model is modest, for the higher-resolution screen and better controls, but the higher-specification screen shows its low-cost nature more. It's a better monitor, but only by a hair's-breadth.
