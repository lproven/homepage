Samsung SyncMaster 17GLi

Verdict: A very pleasing monitor, but the price is a bit much - though street prices may be lower.

Price: £599

Supplier: Samsung 0181-391-0168

Samsung supplied two monitors for this month's Labs, both SyncMaster 17s: this is the 17GLi, and next up is the slightly higher-specification Glsi. The GLi is the most basic model, but the differences are quite small: externally, almost the only sign is that the signal cable is captive on this version, and there are no BNC connectors.

Both units come in the same casing, which is quite small and light, although at over 2in the bezel around the screen is rather wider than is necessary. The monitor looks quite attractive, with a decorative blue strip around the edge of the bezel, and cutouts in the stand, which has a light action but a small range. The main controls - power, brightness and contrast - are on the front panel, with a central drop-down section for the image adjustments.

Although the controls are on-screen, they're not chosen from menus - there are separate buttons for sizing, positioning, distortion, colour control and finally reset and degauss. Pressing these gives an on-screen indicator which can be adjusted using a cursor diamond - fast and easy.

The monitor was correctly identified by Windows 95, but the default driver only offered a disappointing maximum resolution of 1024x768. As it was one of the screens on Matrox' supported list, we chose that, and were rewarded with a stable 1152x864 @ 70Hz mode and a less usable 1280x1024 @ 60Hz. Although the shadow-mask CRT's dot pitch is only a coarse 0.28mm, the image was sharp and bright even at 1152 pixels across the screen; you could happily use this all day.

This is an appealing screen; its picture is good, and it looks small and attractive. The controls are easy and flexible. The only snag is its cost - at a recommended retail of just shy of £600, there are better-specified screens for less. Shop around.
