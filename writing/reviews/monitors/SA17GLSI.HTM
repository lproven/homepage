Samsung SyncMaster 17GLsi

Verdict: A very good screen, but overpriced compared to the competition.

Price: £699

Supplier: Samsung 0181-391-0168

The second Samsung this month is the higher-end GLsi model. Strikingly similar to the lower-end model, this one has a finer 0.26mm dot pitch, a separate D-Sub connector with a discrete signal lead, as well as five BNC connectors. The drop-down control panel gains two extra buttons, too: colour adjustment and BNC or D-Sub input selection. Externally, that's it; the appearance is identical, although when you pick it up you notice an extra kilogram.

The higher-quality picture tube doesn't make a massive difference: picture quality is high on both, and the finer dot pitch isn't very apparent, although the better screen doesn't suffer as much from moiré patterning as its sibling. Both gave excellent results in the screen regulation test, with no visible movement at all, which is exceptional. The GLsi also has a higher video bandwidth, but the standard refresh rates are the same, so this is of little practical value to most users. What is more valuable is the three-year onsite warranty offered by Samsung; this is longer than many suppliers, and may be enough to justify the rather high price for some. As far as documentation goes, the manuals for the two models are only slightly different. Although they're bulked out by containing four different language versions, they are clear and helpful.

The differences between the GLi and GLsi seem comparitively minor for £100: Iiyama manage an aperture-grill CRT for less, and the GLsi can't quite match that. This is a good monitor, but the cost is a little high for what you get. If this were the cheaper model, and there was a £700 Trinitron, then Samsung would probably be the monitors of choice, but they are still worth a look.
