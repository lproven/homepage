Iiyama MF-8617E VisionMaster 17

Verdict: A sound, if basic, choice - a clear display, good connectivity and controls which are almost over-simple

Price: £575

Supplier: Iiyama 01483-745482

Iiyama monitors are a popular choice, many PC manufacturers bundling the 17in models. There are two models - this standard version, and the £75 dearer Pro model with a Trinitron screen, but the shadow-mask version is still crisp and sharp, with a better-than-average 0.26mm dot pitch. The viewable area is a reasonable 400mm from corner to corner, and is fairly flat and square, but like many others, the anti-reflective coating isn't particularly effective and merely turns the reflections purple.

Both Iiyamas are equipped with both kinds of connector, the ordinary SVGA D-Sub and five separate BNC connectors. Unusually, Iiyama supplied us with both types of cable, so we used the BNC, as this gives slightly better picture quality. One side-effect of the BNC cable is that Windows 95's VESA DCC autodetection doesn't work - the BNC cable doesn't have the data lines of the D-Sub, which the monitor uses to identify itself to the computer. However, the VisionMaster 17 is on Windows 95's standard list, so we chose it manually, and were rewarded with a sharp and steady picture with an 85MHz refresh rate.

All adjustments are made via on-screen menus, even brightness and contrast, which is irritating. The menus are simple to operate, but there are only three buttons - menu, plus and minus - which is fiddly. Other factors are acceptable: the stand pivots easily, and the cables are recessed by nearly 10cm - they're tricky to insert, but the unit will sit flush against a wall. Generally, it's a good monitor, but not quite as sharp as the only slightly costlier Pro model.
