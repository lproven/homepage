Watford Aries Professional Media PC

Supplier: Watford Electronics 01582-487777

Price: £1,338 (all in); £1,130 (no sound system); £1,032 (no sound / modem)

Verdict: A lavish bundle with an impressive warranty, but it's neither very fast or very cheap.

Watford has interpreted our system specification slightly differently from most of the other suppliers, including a 16-bit soundcard with powered speakers and an external modem in the bundle. This pushes the price up slightly, and as we didn't ask for them, they have not been reviewed.

Whilst it's not normally where we'd start a system review, the first thing that struck us about this system when we unpacked it was its keyboard, and then its mouse. The keyboard is an interesting device: it comes with a detachable wrist-rest that hooks onto the front. The feel isn't great, but it's very comfortable to use. Another oddity is the extra button at the top right of the keyboard, just like the power key on an Apple Macintosh. This is a reset button - not a hard reset, it merely simulates Ctrl-Alt-Del. In Windows 95, this pops-up the Close Program dialog box, while in DOS it restarts the PC. This is neat, and no more superfluous than the "Windows 95 keys" - which it also has. The mouse is a pleasant change, too; it's the same left-handed model as Hi-Grade's.

The rest of the PC is less idiosyncratic. The case is a modest minitower, only enlivened by a bright red reset button. Although it's quite well-equipped with drive bays, one minor failing is that a front-panel 3.5in drive bay is occupied by the Seagate ST51270A 1.2Gb hard disk, while there's a free bay under the power supply - so if you wanted to fit a removable 3.5in drive, you'd have to move the HD yourself. Otherwise, the interior is neat and tidy, with the neat touch of expansion slot blanking plates that just clip into place without screws - fine, until you fit a card, when you have to find a retaining screw from somewhere. Graphics are provided by a basic but serviceable GrafixStar 300 driving a modest Mitac 15in monitor.

Windows 95 has been slightly customised, with support details in the System control panel and a photograph of Watford's HQ as wallpaper, but this didn't extend to the Win95 service pack or bus-mastering drivers. All the software for the soundcard and modem were correctly in place, though, which is good to see. There's also a bundle of 10 CD titles, which wasn't supplied, whilst a copy of MS Office 95 will cost £149 extra.

Overall, this is a good package, especially when the excellent 6 year warranty is taken into account. Watford's already been around for 20 years, so it should survive to honour this, too. However, the performance isn't stunning, and the price is quite high.
